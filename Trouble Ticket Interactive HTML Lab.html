Interactive Lab: Tablet Running Slow

Scenario: An employee reports their tablet is running slow. As a help desk technician, you must troubleshoot, identify the cause, and document the resolution following CompTIA 220-1202 objectives.

Step 1: Gather Information

You ask the employee: "When did the slowness start?" They respond: "After yesterday’s system update."

What is your first troubleshooting step?

Document the symptoms and verify the issue. Ask clarifying questions about apps, battery, and update timing.

Step 2: Establish a Theory

You suspect system services may be running slowly due to the update.

Where would you check?

Check Task Manager or system monitoring tools for CPU, memory, and background services. Look for update-related processes consuming resources.

Step 3: Test the Theory

You confirm that the update service is consuming high CPU and memory.

What action should you take?

Allow the update to complete or restart the tablet. If necessary, stop/restart the update service safely.

Step 4: Establish a Plan of Action

Plan: Restart the tablet, ensure updates finish, and optimize startup apps.

What CompTIA objective does this align with?

CompTIA 220-1202 Objective: Troubleshoot common mobile OS and application issues, including performance problems.

Step 5: Document the Ticket

Help Desk Ticket

Issue: Tablet running slow after system update.

Steps Taken: Verified symptoms, checked system services, identified update service consuming resources.

Resolution: Restarted tablet, allowed update to complete, optimized startup apps.

Status: Closed – Employee confirmed tablet performance improved.

Step 6: Close the Ticket

Always confirm with the employee that the issue is resolved before closing the ticket.

What should you include in closure notes?

Include resolution steps, confirmation from the employee, and any preventive recommendations.
